# TNV Category Mapping — 9 Top-Level Categories to Federal Accounts

This document maps the 9 categories displayed on the default Tax Dollar form to the underlying federal departments, agencies, and accounts they represent. Citizens using the simple form allocate at the top level; citizens who want finer granularity can expand any line to allocate to specific accounts. The CBO aggregates submissions at whatever level was used and rolls them up to the 9 categories for the published People's Budget.

Allocations reflect approximate FY2024 enacted discretionary spending. Mandatory spending (Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid, interest on the debt) is excluded — TNV operates only on the discretionary budget, currently approximately $1.75 trillion of total federal spending.

---

## 1. Defense — 51.0%

**Department of Defense (DoD)** plus defense-related accounts in other departments.

Includes:
- Military personnel (pay, housing, healthcare for active duty, reserves, National Guard)
- Operation and maintenance (training, base operations, equipment maintenance)
- Procurement (aircraft, ships, missiles, weapons systems)
- Research, Development, Test & Evaluation (RDT&E)
- Military construction and family housing
- Atomic energy defense activities (National Nuclear Security Administration — DoE)
- Defense-related FBI counterintelligence
- Defense health program (Tricare for military families)
- Special Operations Command (SOCOM)

Notable accounts: U.S. Army, U.S. Navy, U.S. Air Force, U.S. Marine Corps, U.S. Space Force, Defense-Wide activities, Defense Health Agency.

---

## 2. Veterans Affairs (VA) — 17.0%

**Department of Veterans Affairs.**

Includes:
- Veterans Health Administration (VHA) — VA hospitals, clinics, mental health services
- Veterans Benefits Administration (VBA) — disability compensation, pensions, GI Bill education, home loans
- National Cemetery Administration (NCA) — military cemeteries and burial benefits
- Medical research at VA facilities
- Caregiver support programs
- Vet centers for combat veterans
- Construction of VA medical facilities

Notable accounts: Medical Services, Medical Community Care, Medical Facilities, Compensation & Pensions, Readjustment Benefits, Information Technology.

---

## 3. Education — 5.0%

**Department of Education** plus education-related programs across other agencies.

Includes:
- K-12 funding (Title I for low-income districts, IDEA special education)
- Pell Grants and federal student aid programs
- Federal student loan administration
- Career and technical education (Perkins funding)
- English language learner programs
- Impact Aid (federal-impacted school districts)
- Education research (Institute of Education Sciences)
- Civil rights enforcement in schools
- Indian education (BIE schools — funded through Interior)
- Head Start (technically under HHS but education-focused)

Notable accounts: Education for the Disadvantaged, Special Education, Student Financial Assistance, Higher Education, Office of Federal Student Aid.

---

## 4. Health — 6.0%

**Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)** discretionary programs. Note: Medicare and Medicaid are mandatory spending and NOT in this category.

Includes:
- National Institutes of Health (NIH) — medical research across 27 institutes
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) — public health, disease surveillance
- Food and Drug Administration (FDA) — drug/device approval, food safety
- Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA) — community health centers, rural health
- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA)
- Indian Health Service (IHS) — healthcare for tribes
- Administration for Children and Families (ACF) — discretionary portion (TANF is mandatory)
- Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ)
- Public Health Emergency Preparedness
- Maternal and Child Health programs

Notable accounts: NIH, CDC, FDA, HRSA, SAMHSA, IHS.

## 5. Infrastructure — 6.0%

**Department of Transportation (DOT)** plus infrastructure programs in other departments.

Includes:
- Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) — highways, bridges, road safety
- Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) — air traffic control, airports, aviation safety
- Federal Transit Administration (FTA) — buses, subways, light rail, commuter rail
- Federal Railroad Administration (FRA) — passenger and freight rail
- National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) — vehicle safety standards
- Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) — trucking safety
- Maritime Administration (MARAD) — ports and shipping
- Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration (PHMSA)
- Army Corps of Engineers Civil Works — flood control, navigation, harbors (technically DoD)
- Water infrastructure (EPA State Revolving Funds for drinking water, wastewater)
- Broadband deployment programs (NTIA — Commerce)
- Power grid modernization (DoE Office of Electricity)

Notable accounts: FHWA, FAA, FTA, FRA, Army Corps Civil Works, EPA State Revolving Funds.

## 6. Science — 5.0%

Federal science, space exploration, and basic research not covered by NIH (which is under Health).

Includes:
- National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) — space exploration, Earth science, aeronautics
- National Science Foundation (NSF) — basic research grants across all non-medical sciences
- Department of Energy Office of Science — physics, fusion, supercomputing, climate science
- National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) — measurement standards, AI safety, cybersecurity research
- National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) — weather, oceans, climate, fisheries
- U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) — earthquakes, water, minerals, mapping
- Smithsonian Institution — research and museums
- Library of Congress research
- Federal STEM education programs across agencies

Notable accounts: NASA, NSF, DoE Office of Science, NIST, NOAA, USGS.

## 7. Environment — 2.5%

Environmental protection, climate response, and natural resource conservation.

Includes:
- Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) — air, water, toxic substances, climate
- Department of Interior natural resource agencies:
  - National Park Service (NPS)
  - U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service (FWS)
  - Bureau of Land Management (BLM) conservation programs
- U.S. Forest Service (USDA) — national forests, wildfire management
- Council on Environmental Quality (CEQ)
- Department of Energy renewable energy programs (Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy)
- Climate research portions of NOAA (overlap with Science)
- Superfund cleanup
- Clean Air Act enforcement
- Clean Water Act enforcement
- Endangered Species Act administration

Notable accounts: EPA Operating Programs, EPA Superfund, NPS, FWS, BLM, USFS, DoE EERE.

## 8. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) — 3.0%

**Department of Homeland Security.**

Includes:
- Customs and Border Protection (CBP) — border security, ports of entry
- Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) — interior enforcement, detention, deportation
- U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) — visa and citizenship processing (largely fee-funded)
- Transportation Security Administration (TSA) — airport security
- U.S. Coast Guard — maritime security, search and rescue, drug interdiction
- Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) — disaster response and recovery
- U.S. Secret Service — protection of officials, financial crime investigation
- Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) — critical infrastructure protection
- Federal Law Enforcement Training Centers (FLETC)
- Science and Technology Directorate

Notable accounts: CBP, ICE, USCIS, TSA, Coast Guard, FEMA, Secret Service, CISA.

## Notes on Granularity

**Default form:** 9 sliders summing to 100%. Designed so a citizen can complete a Tax Dollar in under five minutes without prior research.

**Expanded form:** Any of the 9 categories can be expanded to show its component accounts. A citizen passionate about NASA can drill into Science and allocate within it: NASA vs NSF vs DoE Office of Science vs NOAA. A citizen who served can drill into VA and allocate among healthcare vs benefits vs cemeteries vs research.

**Templates:** Advocacy organizations publish pre-filled Tax Dollars at various granularity. A defense industry template might detail Defense down to specific programs (F-35, Columbia-class submarine, hypersonics R&D). An environmental template might detail Environment down to specific EPA programs (Superfund cleanup, Clean Air Act enforcement) and Science contributions (NOAA climate research). Citizens adopt templates with one click, modify any line they care about, and submit.

**Aggregation:** The CBO aggregates submissions at their declared level of granularity. A submission detailing Science down to specific agencies counts at both the detailed level (for the detailed published breakdown) and the rolled-up Science total (for the 9-category headline People's Budget).

**Excluded from TNV:** Mandatory spending — Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid, interest on the debt, federal civilian and military retirement obligations. These are set by formula in existing law and cannot be changed through the annual appropriations process. TNV operates only on what Congress actually votes on each year.

---

## Implementation Notes

The 9-category structure mirrors how OMB and CBO already report discretionary budget data. Existing reporting infrastructure can feed directly into the People's Budget aggregation. The category boundaries are consistent with the budget functions used in the Federal Budget appropriations process — Function 050 (National Defense) maps cleanly to Defense, Function 700 (Veterans Benefits) maps cleanly to VA, etc.

The "Other" category exists because no clean 9-category breakdown captures everything, and a 20-category form is too complex for the average citizen. The expanded form gives motivated citizens the granularity they want; the simple form gives the rest of the population a usable interface.
